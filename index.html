Team History

Summary

The Minnesota Timberwolves, established in 1989 as an NBA expansion team, are based in Minneapolis, Minnesota. The franchise struggled in its early years but gained prominence in the late 1990s and early 2000s with the arrival of Kevin Garnett, who became the team’s cornerstone. Garnett led the Timberwolves to their most successful era, highlighted by an appearance in the Western Conference Finals in 2004, where they were led by Garnett, Sam Cassell, and Latrell Sprewell. Despite Garnett’s departure in 2007 and a subsequent rebuilding phase, the Timberwolves have showcased talented players over the years, including Kevin Love, Karl-Anthony Towns, and Anthony Edwards. The team returned to the playoffs in 2018 and has been working to establish itself as a consistent contender in the competitive Western Conference.
